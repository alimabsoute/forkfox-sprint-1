Description
2000 char max
ForkFox is an AI-powered iOS app that scores individual dishes at restaurants — not the restaurants themselves. The food internet has been stuck on restaurant-level scoring for 20 years (Yelp, Google, OpenTable, Resy all rate the place, not the plate). The actual question — what should I order? — has been left to gut, friends, and review-mining. ForkFox solves that with a patent-pending algorithm that reads thousands of reviews per restaurant, extracts dish-level signal, and scores each dish 0-100 on four axes: quality, value, execution, and consistency. As of May 2026 we've scored 3,600+ dishes across Philadelphia and the San Francisco Bay Area. The score is personalized — your 94 won't match your cousin's 94, because palates differ and ForkFox learns yours from the dishes you rate over time. Real numbers from the data: 47 ramen bowls scored across 12 Philly restaurants. 89 BYO entrees scored across the East Passyunk and Italian Market corridors. 134 brunch dishes across the Mission and Marina. Beta users average 11-minute sessions, vs the 2-3 minute industry average for food apps — that gap is the product proof. Currently iOS-only via TestFlight (Android and App Store forthcoming). Free beta — sign up at forkfox.ai and the TestFlight invite arrives within 24 hours. Founded 2026 by Ali Mabsoute (ali@forkfox.ai) and engineering co-founder Nik. Headquartered in Philadelphia. Building the dish-level layer the food internet has been missing.
