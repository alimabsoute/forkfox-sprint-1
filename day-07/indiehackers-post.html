Building ForkFox — 6 months in, here's what we've learned about dish-level food scoring

Hey IH, My co-founder Nik and I have been building ForkFox since late 2025. Shipping the iOS TestFlight beta now. Want to share what we learned, what's working, what's not, and what we'd do differently. **The product** ForkFox scores individual dishes at restaurants — not the restaurants themselves. We use a patent-pending algorithm to read thousands of reviews per restaurant, extract dish-level signal, and score each dish 0-100 on four axes: quality, value, execution, and consistency. As of May 2026: 3,600+ dishes scored across Philadelphia and the San Francisco Bay Area. **Why we built it** The food internet has been stuck on restaurant-level scoring for 20 years. Yelp, Google, OpenTable, Resy, Beli all rate the place. The actual question — what should I order — has been left to gut, friends, and review-mining. We thought there was a real wedge in scoring the plate, not the place. **What's working** - **Engagement is real.** Beta users average 11-minute sessions. Industry average for food apps is 2-3 minutes. When people find ForkFox, they stay. - **Personalization works.** Your 94 won't match your cousin's 94 because palates differ. We solve cold-start with ~5 dish ratings — after that the personalized score is meaningfully different from the average. - **The pSEO engine is compounding.** We've published 30+ neighborhood + cuisine guides on forkfox.ai/carte/. Three weeks in they're not ranking yet (Google takes 3-6 months on a new domain), but the asset is there. **What's not working** - **TestFlight is brutal.** App Store install is 3 steps and 15 seconds. TestFlight is 6 steps and 2-3 minutes. Conversion drops from ~10-15% (App Store benchmark) to 3-5% (TestFlight reality). Every paid ad we run costs 2-3x what it would post-App-Store. - **Acquisition is a different muscle than building.** I'm an engineer who can also do marketing. Nik is an engineer. Neither of us are full-time marketers. We're learning that building marketing infrastructure (landing pages, copy kit, ad creative, Reddit accounts with karma history, influencer relationships) takes months of consistent work BEFORE paid spend produces ROI. - **B2B is harder than we thought.** ForkFox has a restaurant-owner side at forkfox.ai/business. Getting owners to claim their listing requires real outreach, not just "build it and they'll come." We have ~5 founding restaurant partners interested; we want 20+ before App Store launch. **The numbers** - Domain age: 3 weeks - Articles published (pSEO): 34+ - Beta signups so far: ~30 (mostly Bay Area through warm network) - Real Philadelphia visitors in last 14 days (audited from GA4): 1 - Spend on paid acquisition to date: $0 (Meta restriction was lifted last week, ads about to start) - Bot share of GA4 traffic: 64% (Meta link-preview crawlers from data-center cities) **What we'd do differently** - **Set up Meta ads on Day 1**, not Day 60. The Meta Business restriction took 2 weeks to clear and burned half our pre-launch window. - **Build the /beta landing page before any paid spend.** We're about to launch ads and just realized we don't have a dedicated TestFlight install landing page. That's a Day-Negative-1 task that should have been Day 1. - **Pick one city, not two.** Philly + Bay Area sounds great in the deck. In practice, the 2-person team can only credibly compete in one geography in the short term. We should have been Philly-only for the first 60 days. - **Don't burn one-shot platforms early.** We were tempted to launch on Product Hunt during TestFlight. We didn't. Saving PH + Hacker News + major press for the App Store launch (when conversion is 3x higher). **What's next** - 14-day acquisition sprint starting Mon May 12 - App Store submission targeted for late June - Product Hunt launch the day the App Store version goes live If you want to try the beta: forkfox.ai (free, TestFlight invite within 24 hours). If you have feedback on the build journey or want to talk shop, ali@forkfox.ai. Thanks IH for being a place where you can post the actual numbers. — Ali
